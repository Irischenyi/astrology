All planets rest when they retrograde. (The Sun and moon do not retrograde, and they are not planets—the Sun is a star, and the moon is the Earth’s satellite.) The planets that orbit closest to Earth are the ones we feel the strongest when retrograde, and those are Mercury, Mars, and Venus, which astrologers call the “personal planets.” These planets make every day seem a little different, while the big, outer planets—Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto—affect entire generations because they move so slowly and are in one sign for years. They also affect you, only not as much when retrograde, not like the smaller personal planets. Jupiter is in the “Goldilocks,” medium, region, moving just enough to set a goal and see it materialize during his one-year visit.

As you may have guessed, this will give 2025 a slow start, but there is a plus side to all these retrograde periods. They will give us all time to reflect on past events, actions, and decisions. With the benefit of hindsight, you can see what went right, what went wrong, what you learned, and what you might need to tweak. We all like to continually move forward, but the universe advises us not to rush ahead so much all the time. Reflection is just as important as action.

Christmas Day and Chanukah will fall on the same day this year, December 25. The transiting moon will appear in the deeply spiritual, emotional, and privacy-seeking sign of Scorpio. Surround yourself with those who matter to you the most for a peaceful, rejuvenative holiday.

This new moon will bring a flurry of assignments to focus on, so you won’t be bored.
