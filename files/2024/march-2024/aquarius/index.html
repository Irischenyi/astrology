March will be all about looking at your finances. You may be gathering receipts to show your accountant to prepare your tax return, examining your spending to see areas you can cut back on, and studying your savings and investments to see how they are performing.

You may be hoping to get a raise, but you’ve had Saturn in your financial second house, which rules earned income and salary. Saturn has been with you for a year since he first entered this area of your chart in March 2023. Saturn constricts, limits, and tightens everything it touches. We all go through Saturn’s bootcamp with his tough-love way of teaching, and you still have two years to go until he will be gone in February 2026.

Once gone, Saturn will not return to this earned salary portion of your chart until 2052 to 2055, and you will carry the financial wisdom you are learning now throughout the coming nearly three decades. Saturn is a little like self-adjusting shampoo—some people need a little, some people need a lot. Saturn will teach you only what you need to know. If you experienced Saturn in 1993 to 1996 when he was last in Pisces (your financial house), this visit from Saturn will be easier, as theoretically you learned a great deal last time and will only need a refresher course now.

A new moon, which will come on March 10 in your second financial house, usually opens up opportunity, but as said, Saturn standing close by in your financial house is spoiling the pudding for now. Any raise you receive would be so scant as to be insignificant. Quitting your job for another one won’t make life any better either, for the same circumstances will apply—they are caused by Saturn. Saturn will follow you, so you might as well figure out how to make your present job work better for you.
