Your Horoscope for Pisces

by Susan Miller

It’s birthday time of the year, and all the little planets are sending greetings to you. They want to make sure you have a happy birthday year ahead. You have more control over events now than you might assume you do, as all the planets are on the eastern side of the chart, favoring you. This also suggests you do not need a middleman, agent, or anyone to get things rolling for you—you are the engine, capable of starting events.

Saturn entered Pisces in March 2023, and now you have Saturn conjunct the Sun, which is an exceedingly rare aspect. The last time you had Saturn in Pisces was from 1993 to 1996. Last year, 2023, Saturn focused on February-born Pisces, and this year it will be those born March 1 to 10 who will be feeling Saturn’s influence the most. The last time those Pisces were highlighted by Saturn directly was in 1995, and that could have been a year of great accomplishment. Perhaps you were not yet born, but if you were, think back and see if you can find a theme.

You may have done something to change your status, something that you had to be ready to do and showed that you were becoming more mature. You may have done one of the following: started a business or been given a big promotion, received your college or master’s degree, married, published a book, had a baby, bought your first house, or chose something else that required effort and learning to accomplish. Now decades later, Saturn is back to give you a refresher course. He can only come to see you every 29.5 years, because that is how long he takes to revolve around the Sun and return to the same constellation. Think of Saturn as a high-priced consultant who can only work with you rarely, but whose wisdom you will need and will carry with you in the intervening nearly three decades, until he can come back to see you again.
