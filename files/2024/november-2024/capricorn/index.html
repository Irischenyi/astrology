Your Horoscope for Capricorn

by Susan Miller

This will be a generally soft, romantic, and fun month, which might sound surprising to you because October was such a strong month for professional decisions, advancement, and matters related to your home and family. Each month the universe strives to bring balance to your life. After a period that emphasized career, home, family, and perhaps some decision-making, you will now move into an easier, breezier month that allows you to exhale and enjoy life.

The new moon in Scorpio, 10 degrees, will arrive immediately on November 1. This new moon will light your sector of hopes and wishes (eleventh house), that magical part of your chart where you’ll be reminded that dreams can indeed come true. You will have time to think about what you would love to achieve in your coming birthday year, which will be upon you as soon as the Sun moves into Capricorn on December 21.

This new moon on November 1 will initiate a very social time for you, when friends will start to call you with news of upcoming holiday parties and get-togethers. You could also be making arrangements with friends to have a quick dinner after work or a leisurely brunch over the weekend. All this social activity will be noticeable directly after the new moon arrives on November 1 and in the weeks ahead. You need time to have fun and relax, so indulge a little.

The part about this new moon that will be so delicious is that your ruling planet Saturn will be sending greetings to the new moon in a heavenly “trine” aspect, which is the best, most harmonious aspect you can wish to see. Saturn will be in your third house, which suggests that you might be hoping to go on a quick trip before the holidays sweep in and take center stage. Short-distance travel will be possible, and it looks as though you may be visiting your sister, brother, or cousin. Pluto will send travel planet Mercury a high-five to help make that trip possible, if you want to go.

Saturn’s involvement also suggests that an older, mature friend could give you very good advice on a current dilemma this month. New friends (of any age) that you might make now are likely to stay in your life for a long time. Try to get out of the house a little more by attending events, and be open to expanding your circle of acquaintances. You might want to join a charity event, community activity, or humanitarian effort that will bring you in contact with like-minded people. You are always interested in finding ways to stay up to date on changes in your industry, so consider attending a conference, seminar, or convention that might be going on in November.

Capricorns who will benefit the very most from this new moon on November 1 and in the weeks ahead are those who were born within five days of December 31 or those who have Capricorn rising or their natal moon in Capricorn between 5 and 15 degrees. If you have a natal planet in Cancer, Scorpio, Pisces, Taurus, Virgo, or Capricorn between 5 and 15 degrees in your birth chart, you will also benefit in a big way—look at the planet and see the house it occupies in your chart. After the new moon arrives, that planet will help you a great deal in whichever area it occupies.

Now let’s look at Venus, the planet in charge of your fifth house of true love. Good news! Venus will move into Capricorn on November 11 and stay until December 6, making your charm and charisma more noticeable and engaging. This is the perfect time to choose new pieces and accessories for your wardrobe and to investigate new ways to style your hair (men, too). Women might want to shop for new seasonal makeup colors, and both sexes might want to explore a new scent for the coming season during the time Venus will spend in Capricorn.

All Capricorns have a chance to find love and fun this month, whether at social events or through surprising romantic encounters.

Only a few days after Venus enters Capricorn, the full moon on November 15 will appear in Taurus at 24 degrees. That full moon will brilliantly light your fifth house of true love for the first time this year. If you are single and hoping to meet your one truelove, this full moon is rich with possibilities.

The full moon on November 15 in Taurus (a compatible sign for you) will conjunct Uranus, the planet of surprise, so almost anything could happen. Uranus prides himself on his ability to bring unpredictable events, so I can’t say which way things will go. Uranus will simultaneously oppose the Sun in your friendship house, so you may have a problem with a friend, or a friend may be hit with some difficult news. Aside from what I just said, I CAN say that if you meet someone new romantically, the chemistry will be very strong and magnetic between you. When Uranus is involved, it is important to go slowly. You might not want to take things slow and easy, but you must if you want this relationship to last—that’s important to remember when you are dealing with Uranus.

If you have already found the person you love, you will enjoy this full moon, too. You will be more likely to find fun and romantic things to do together. You can take a long weekend out of town or have a late-night dinner in a restaurant you’ve always wanted to go to. If you love to cook, take out your fine china and surprise your partner with their favorite dish. Finding time to talk and focus on one another will be a delight during this full moon, so go ahead—linger over your meal. Giving your full attention to anyone is the greatest gift you could ever give that person.

All Capricorns have a chance to find love and fun at this full moon. Those who might get a double dip of pleasure include those born on January 14, plus or minus five days. The same is true if you have either Capricorn rising or your natal moon in Capricorn at 24 degrees, plus or minus five degrees.

Now let’s turn to look at Pluto, a powerhouse planet that is in his last days of Capricorn in our lifetime. You have become used to having this power planet behind you, as Pluto first entered Capricorn in January 2008. He will leave permanently this month on November 19 after 16 years in Capricorn. You might miss his presence, for you had the chance to become quite a force of nature in your chosen profession or your community during that time. Now Pluto will head to your second house of earned income, which is a house that covers your savings and possessions. You now have a chance to save and invest wisely to create quite a bit of wealth.

Pluto will not be back to Capricorn until February 2254, and no, that is not a typo. Pluto takes 248 years to revolve through all 12 zodiac signs, which is the reason the world will not see Pluto return to Capricorn anytime soon. Almost immediately after Pluto arrived in Capricorn in January 2008, the world saw a financial crisis of epic proportions, and Pluto moved to clean up some faulty structures in the banking industry’s practices. Now Pluto will be in Aquarius, ushering in the digital era and firmly presenting the long-awaited Age of Aquarius. Due to his slow orbit, Pluto is considered one of the most powerful of all planets, for he remains in each sign for a very long time, which can influence the theme of an entire generation. Inclusion, accessibility, and fairness to every person will become dominant themes.

The trend toward working in a more mobile way, with companies offering hybrid methods of working, will increase in some industries. (Not all industries can allow their employees to work from home during some days of the week, but those that can allow it, will.) Another change will be that conspicuous consumption will no longer hold the fascination that it once did for society—it won’t matter if you have the bigger house or the more expensive car than your neighbors or friends. What will matter is what you know and what you will share with others to make the world a better place—that will more strongly affect your status. Travel will become easier, faster, and possibly cheaper.

It never takes Pluto long to assert his presence in a new sign. During Pluto’s past tour of Capricorn, it was the king or president who set the agenda and told the populace what to do. In his writings, the Greek philosopher Plato called Capricorn “the philosopher king,” by which he meant that under Capricorn, the king or president took good care of the people in his care. With Aquarius, the picture is flipped: The people tell the king or president what they need, and in so doing, the people set the agenda. Every sign in the zodiac is in many ways the opposite of the sign that comes before it, and this is the way balance is kept in the horoscope.
