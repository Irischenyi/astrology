Your Horoscope for Leo

by Susan Miller

You have an extraordinary chart in November. It will seem like nearly every part of your life is vying for attention—it’s a good, solid month where you can make a lot of progress. Developments in your home, property, or family life along with unusual, and possibly great, news about your career will make this a busy, productive month. You will also have time to discover new love or find fresh ways to enjoy romance with your present partner. In all, this has every earmark of being a month to remember.

Let’s start with the highly friendly new moon that begins the month on November 1 in Scorpio, 10 degrees, which will appear in your home and family sector. In the days that follow, you may decide to give your apartment or house an upgraded look for the coming holidays. If you move or renovate, you will likely not need or want to find a new place again for a very long time, possibly forever—you will like your space too much to leave. The reason I say this is because Saturn will give you his gift of stability, heighten your sense of security, and bring you long-term gain.

If you are looking for a perfect piece of furniture, ideal decorations, or beautiful tableware to help you celebrate the holidays, you can find it because Uranus will give you that element of luck to come across precisely what you want. Of course, if you’ve been hoping to locate the right new space to move into, this is the new moon of the year to accomplish your goal.

Sometimes when we have a new moon in the domestic sector, we don’t use it to improve our living situation but instead direct this energy to assist a family member, most likely a parent or child who needs advice, attention, and loving care. This is a new moon, so you may be setting up a new situation for one of your parents—hiring a nurses aid or finding an assisted living facility are among the many possibilities that will fit your parent’s needs. Or you may be searching for a new school for your child or tutor who can give your child special lessons on a topic he or she has been struggling with.
